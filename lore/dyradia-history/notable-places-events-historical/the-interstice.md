## **Overview**

**Type:** Liminal Transit Phenomenon  
**Originator:** Bohrova  
**Status:** Partially Functional (Post-Fracture)  
**Access:** Regulated, Restricted, or Lost (Varies by Region)

The **Interstice** is a stabilized liminal space existing between the material world of Dyradia and the planar domain associated with the Dyra. It was engineered as a means of long-distance travel, allowing individuals and caravans to traverse vast distances in a fraction of the time required by conventional routes.

Contrary to popular belief, travelers do not enter the Dyra plane itself. Instead, they pass through a **contained in-between space**, where spatial relationships differ from those of the material world.

---

## **Function and Structure**

### **Transit Principles**

- Distance within the Interstice does not correspond linearly to distance in Dyradia
    
- Travel is directional and guided via anchored nodes
    
- Time passage is generally consistent, though deviations have been recorded
    
- Entry and exit points are fixed unless destabilized
    

The Interstice is accessed through **anchored thresholds**, commonly referred to as _gates_, _spans_, or _nodes_. These structures were placed strategically across Dyradia during the height of Bohrovaâ€™s influence.
